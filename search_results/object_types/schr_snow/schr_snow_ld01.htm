Snow knives Object list

Snow knives (qitchuaġvik) were used to cut blocks of snow used for building snow houses and for drinking water. They could also be used as snow beaters, to brush snow off the clothing.

In contrast to drill bows, snow knives can be recognized by their flat blades, tapered points, and a handle providing a firm grip. Before easy access to metal after contact with European explorers and traders, the blades were usually made from the ivory of walrus tusks.
